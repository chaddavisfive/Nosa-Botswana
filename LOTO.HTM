Lock-Out Procedure

On completion of this course the learner will be able to explain and apply Lock-Out Tag-Out Systems to control hazardous energy. Identify isolation points for the isolation of energy from the system (a machine, equipment, or process) which physically locks the system in a safe mode. Identify all the energy sources including electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, chemical, thermal, or other sources in machines and equipment which can be hazardous to workers. Be familiar with a Job Safety Analysis and Permit to Work to be completed before servicing and maintenance of machines and equipment, to prevent the unexpected startup or release of stored energy that can result in serious injury or death to workers and others.
