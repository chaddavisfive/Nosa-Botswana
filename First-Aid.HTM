First-Aid Level 1

Course Description

First Aid Level 1 equips learners with the essential knowledge and practical skills needed to respond to medical emergencies. Learners will gain the ability to provide immediate care for injuries and sudden illness, stabilise a casualty, and apply basic life-support techniques until professional medical help arrives.

Formative Assessment

Practical demonstrations and scenario-based activities will be completed during class.

Summative Assessment

A written and practical assessment will be conducted at the end of the course.Learners must achieve a minimum of 65%.

Course Outline

On successful completion of the course, learners will be able to perform basic first aid in emergency situations and apply recognised procedures to preserve life, prevent the condition from worsening, and promote recovery.

By the end of the course, you should have a better understanding of the following:

  • What first aid is and the role of a first aider
  • Basic life support principles (CPR and airway management)
  • How to assess an emergency scene safely
  • Management of bleeding, wounds, burns, and fractures
  • Treatment of shock, fainting, and medical emergencies
  • Handling unconscious casualties
  • Safe casualty movement techniques
  • The importance of infection control and personal safety

Course Information

Classroom Duration: 3 days
NQF Level: 1
Pre-Requisites: None
Target Groups: Employees, Supervisors, Safety Representatives, and members of Emergency Response Teams
