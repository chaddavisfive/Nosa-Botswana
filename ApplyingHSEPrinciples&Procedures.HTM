Applying HSE Principles & Procedures

Course Description

The course will enable learners to understand the importance of compliance to Health, Safety and other requirements for Hand and other Tools used in a specific workplace. The importance of integrating Health and Safety into all routine activities at the workplace. In addition learners will be able to identify any deviations pertaining to Health and Safety performance in the workplace from legal and other requirements and be able to ensure that the correct action is taken in situations where Health and Safety issues are not in accordance with requirements.

Formative Assessment

Various activities will be completed during class as well as for homework.

Summative Assessment

Written assessment to be done at the end of the course.
Learners must achieve a minimum of 65%.

Course Outline

By the end of the course, the successful learner will be able to:

  • recognizing hazards pertaining to the storage and use of Hand Tools, Electrical, Fuel Powered, Hydraulic and Powder Actuated Tools
  • Understand the importance of compliance to the requirements, both legal requirements and other in a workplace
  • Integrate Health and Safety considerations into all routine activities in a workplace
  • Identify Health and Safety related deviations in the workplace, as well as deviations related to performance in the workplace (workers and others under the influence of drugs/alcohol)
  • Monitor workplace compliance to Health and Safety requirements, against specified requirements (physical observation and assessment of employee and others using hand and other tools)
  • Remedy and corrective action workplace non-compliance to, and non-performance of Health and Safety requirements and programmes

Course Information

Classroom Duration: 2 days
NQF Level: 2
Pre-Requisites: Communication & Mathematical Literacy at NQF Level 1
Target Groups: Artisans and General workers
