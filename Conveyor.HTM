Conveyor Belt Safety

Course Description

The conveyor belt safety course outline covers the principles of safe operation, proper personal protective equipment (PPE), hazard identification, lockout/tagout procedures, emergency response, and the importance of regular maintenance and cleaning.

Formative Assessment

Various activities will be completed during class as well as for homework.

Summative Assessment

Written assessment and practical exercise to be completed at the end of the course.

Course Outline

By the end of the course, the successful learner will be able to understand:

  • Conveyor Systems and SafetyOverview of different conveyor types and components, Importance of safe operation and risk assessment,Regulatory requirements and company policies
  • Some key health, safety and environment related definitions and issues
  • Key aspects OHS Act and NEMA
  • Important definitions in environmental management
  • The role of management within HSE
  • The benefit of a management system
  • The important role of controls in risk management
  • The function of the hierarchy of controls

Course Information

Unit Standard Title: Learners trained in conveyor belt safety will be able to identify hazards, choose the correct personal protective equipment (PPE), be familiar with the emergency procedures. The training also includes proper operation, maintenance, lockout/tagout procedures, and the use of safety devices like pull cords and guards.

Classroom Duration: 2 days
NQF Level: 3
Pre-Requisites: Communication and English Literacy
Target Groups: Supervisors, Artisans, Plant Operators, Cleaning Staff
